// INVESTMENT PRODUCTS

THE FULL TOOLKIT.

01
CPF (CENTRAL PROVIDENT FUND)[ CAPITAL SAFE ]

OA earns 2.5% p.a., SA earns 4% p.a. Voluntary SA top-ups (up to $8,000/yr) give dollar-for-dollar income tax relief. Use CPFIS to invest OA funds in ETFs above the first $20,000.

MIN — $0  ·  RISK — VERY LOW  ·  RETURNS — GUARANTEED
02
SINGAPORE SAVINGS BONDS (SSB)[ FLEXIBLE ]

Government-backed bonds earning ~3–4% p.a. with stepped interest. Capital-guaranteed. Redeem any month with no penalty. Apply via DBS, OCBC, or UOB iBanking during the monthly window.

MIN — $500  ·  RISK — VERY LOW  ·  RETURNS — ~3–4% P.A.
03
S-REITS (REAL ESTATE INVESTMENT TRUSTS)[ HIGH YIELD ]

Own malls, offices, data centres, hospitals without ABSD or a million-dollar down payment. SGX REITs must distribute 90%+ of income as dividends. Popular: CapitaLand, Mapletree, Frasers, Keppel DC.

MIN — ~$300  ·  RISK — MEDIUM  ·  YIELD — 4–7% P.A.
04
SGX STOCKS & STI ETF[ LOCAL GROWTH ]

Singapore's stock market. Blue chips: DBS, OCBC, UOB, Singtel. The STI ETF (ES3) passively tracks Singapore's 30 largest companies in a single low-cost fund. Ideal for local equity exposure.

MIN — ~$200  ·  RISK — MEDIUM  ·  FEE — 0.30% P.A.
05
ROBO-ADVISORS[ BEGINNER PICK ]

StashAway, Syfe, Endowus — platforms that automatically invest in diversified global portfolios. No stock-picking required. Set your risk level, transfer money, let the algorithm do the rest. From $1.

MIN — $1  ·  RISK — LOW TO MEDIUM  ·  FEE — 0.4–0.8% P.A.
06
T-BILLS (TREASURY BILLS)[ CAPITAL SAFE ]

Short-term Singapore government securities (6-month / 1-year). Recently yielding 3.5–4%. Bid through your bank or use CPF-OA funds. Results announced bi-weekly. Extremely safe.

MIN — $1,000  ·  RISK — VERY LOW  ·  YIELD — ~3.5–4%
07
SRS (SUPPLEMENTARY RETIREMENT SCHEME)[ TAX RELIEF ]

Voluntary retirement scheme offering dollar-for-dollar income tax relief (up to $15,300/yr for citizens/PRs). Invest SRS funds in stocks, ETFs, or unit trusts through your bank.

MIN — $1  ·  TAX RELIEF — UP TO $15,300/YR  ·  RISK — VARIES
08
UNIT TRUSTS / MANAGED FUNDS[ DIVERSIFIED ]

Professionally managed funds via banks or Endowus. Can use CPF and SRS money. Use platforms with 0% sales charge (Endowus, Fundsupermart) to avoid eroding returns with front-end fees.

MIN — $100  ·  RISK — VARIES  ·  FEE — 0.5–2.5% P.A.

// KEY TAX RULE FOR SINGAPORE INVESTORS
The US charges a 30% withholding tax on dividends paid to non-US investors. Singapore has zero capital gains tax — all stock price gains are yours. To cut dividend tax from 30% to 15%, use Ireland-domiciled ETFs (VWRA, CSPX on the London Stock Exchange) instead of US-domiciled ones (VOO, VTI).

// THE SMART APPROACH
USE BOTH MARKETS TOGETHER.
Use Singapore products (CPF, SSB, REITs, T-Bills) for stable income and capital preservation. Use Ireland-domiciled global ETFs (VWRA or CSPX) for long-term growth compounding over decades. This combination gives you steady cash flow from Singapore plus the full power of global equity markets.
📚 — INVESTING CONCEPTS( 101 )
INVESTING
101✦

Master these fundamentals and you'll be better equipped than most retail investors. Click any concept to expand it.

Compound interest means you earn returns on your returns. $1,000 at 7% p.a. becomes $1,070 after year 1. In year 2 you earn 7% on $1,070 — not $1,000. Over decades this creates exponential growth. $200/month from age 25 at 7% grows to over $520,000 by 65. Starting 10 years later reduces that to ~$247,000.

DCA means investing a fixed amount (e.g., $300/month) regardless of market conditions. When prices drop, you buy more units; when prices rise, fewer. Your average cost per unit is smoothed out over time. You remove the emotional pressure of timing the market — and historically this approach beats most active strategies.

Spreading your money across different assets, industries, and countries so one bad investment can't destroy your portfolio. VWRA owns shares in 3,700+ companies across 50+ countries — maximum diversification in a single fund. "Don't put all your eggs in one basket" is the core principle.

An ETF holds many assets but trades on a stock exchange like a single share. Buy one share of VWRA and you own a tiny slice of 3,700 global companies instantly. ETFs are low-cost, transparent, and highly liquid. They combine the diversification of a mutual fund with the ease of trading a stock — ideal for beginners and professionals alike.

How much temporary loss you can handle — emotionally and financially. If you'd panic-sell during a 30% crash, you have low risk tolerance and should hold more bonds and SSBs. If you can hold steady knowing the market historically recovers, you suit a higher-equity portfolio. Your investment horizon matters: longer = more risk you can afford.

When a company or REIT earns profit, it distributes some of that cash to shareholders — called dividends. Singapore REITs yield 4–7% annually. You receive cash payments just for holding the investment — like collecting rent from a property, without the landlord headaches. No dividend tax on Singapore-listed stocks.

The annual fee charged by a fund as a percentage. VWRA charges 0.22% — for every $10,000 invested, you pay $22/yr. Active unit trusts charge 1.5–2.5%, costing you 25%+ of your final portfolio value over 30 years. Always choose the lowest-cost fund that meets your needs.

How you split your portfolio between stocks, bonds, cash, and property. Simple rule: hold your age as a percentage in bonds (at 30, hold 30% bonds, 70% stocks). Younger investors can hold more equities for higher long-term growth. Robo-advisors handle this automatically.

The rising cost of goods and services. Singapore averages ~2–3% p.a. If your savings account pays 0.1% but inflation is 3%, your money loses 2.9% of its purchasing power each year. $100,000 today will only buy what ~$74,000 buys in 10 years. Investing is not optional — it's how you protect what you've earned.

If your target is 80% stocks / 20% bonds, but stocks grow and become 90% of your portfolio, you rebalance by selling some stocks and buying more bonds to return to 80/20. Rebalancing enforces "buy low, sell high" automatically. Most investors do this once or twice a year. Robo-advisors handle it for you.

A tax deducted at source before dividends reach your account. The US government charges non-US investors a flat 30% WHT on all dividends from US-listed funds (VOO, VTI, QQQ). To cut this to 15%, use Ireland-domiciled equivalents (VWRA, CSPX, EQQQ) — they benefit from the US–Ireland tax treaty. This difference compounds massively over decades.

CPF is Singapore's mandatory retirement fund. Your Ordinary Account (OA) earns 2.5% and your Special Account (SA) earns 4% — both government-guaranteed. Through the CPF Investment Scheme (CPFIS), you can invest OA funds above $20,000 in approved stocks and ETFs. Key rule: only invest via CPFIS if you're confident of beating 2.5% net of fees and mistakes.

REITs are companies that own income-generating properties and are listed on a stock exchange. Singapore REITs must distribute at least 90% of taxable income as dividends. You receive property income without buying a property — no stamp duty, no mortgage, no tenants. Popular categories: retail malls, industrial/logistics, data centres, hospitals, offices. Current yields: 4–7% p.a.

When you buy a bond, you're lending money to a government or company. In return you receive regular interest (coupon) payments and your principal back at maturity. Singapore Savings Bonds are the safest — capital-guaranteed, government-backed, redeemable anytime. T-Bills are shorter-term (6–12 months). Bonds belong in your portfolio as a stabiliser during equity market crashes.

An optional government scheme where voluntary contributions earn dollar-for-dollar income tax relief — up to $15,300/yr for Singapore citizens and PRs. Money sits in your SRS account at a bank (DBS, OCBC, or UOB) and can be invested in stocks, ETFs, REITs, and unit trusts. Withdrawals at or after 62 attract only 50% taxation. A powerful tool for high earners.

Market cap = share price × total shares outstanding. It's the total value of a company. Large-cap stocks (Apple, DBS) are more stable. Mid-cap and small-cap stocks have higher growth potential but more volatility. Index funds like VWRA weight holdings by market cap — so the biggest companies make up the largest share of the fund.

A valuation metric: share price divided by earnings per share. A P/E of 20 means you're paying $20 for every $1 of annual profit. High P/E = expensive or high-growth expectations. Low P/E = cheap or declining business. The S&P 500 averages ~20–25x P/E. Singapore banks (DBS, OCBC) often trade at 10–14x — considered value stocks.

3–6 months of your living expenses held in cash or a high-yield savings account (e.g., DBS Multiplier, OCBC 360). This is non-negotiable before investing a single dollar. If a crisis hits and you have no emergency fund, you may be forced to sell investments at the worst possible time. Your emergency fund is what allows your investments to stay invested through downturns.

Accumulating funds (e.g. VWRA) reinvest dividends automatically back into the fund — no dividends paid out, maximising compound growth. Distributing funds (e.g. VWRD) pay dividends as cash. For young investors in the accumulation phase, accumulating is better — no need to manually reinvest, and less friction. For retirees who need income, distributing suits better.

Robo-advisors are automated investment platforms that build and manage a diversified portfolio for you based on your risk level. In Singapore: StashAway (globally diversified, proprietary risk model), Syfe (Core portfolios + REIT+ option), Endowus (can use CPF and SRS money). Fees: 0.4–0.8% p.a. Perfect for beginners who want professional-grade diversification without picking stocks. Start from $1.

🔤 — TERMINOLOGY( A–Z )
THE
GLOSSARY✦

Plain-English definitions of every term you'll encounter as a Singapore investor.

→
ASSET ALLOCATION

How your portfolio is split between stocks, bonds, cash, and property. Reflects your age, goals, and risk tolerance.

BEAR MARKET

A market decline of 20%+ from a recent peak. Happens every 3–5 years on average. Long-term investors should hold steady.

BOND

A loan to a government or company in exchange for regular interest payments and principal back at maturity. Lower risk than stocks.

BULL MARKET

A sustained period of rising prices (20%+ from a recent low). The S&P 500 has spent most of its history in bull markets.

CPF (CENTRAL PROVIDENT FUND)

Singapore's mandatory retirement savings system. OA earns 2.5%, SA earns 4%. Voluntary top-ups earn income tax relief.

COMPOUND INTEREST

Earning returns on your returns. The longer your time horizon, the more powerful. The foundation of long-term wealth building.

DOLLAR-COST AVERAGING (DCA)

Investing a fixed dollar amount on a regular schedule regardless of market price. Removes emotion and averages entry cost over time.

DIVIDEND

A cash payment from a company or REIT to shareholders. Tax-free for Singapore-listed stocks and REITs.

DIVERSIFICATION

Spreading investments across assets, industries, and countries so one bad investment doesn't ruin your portfolio.

ETF (EXCHANGE-TRADED FUND)

A fund holding many assets that trades on a stock exchange like a single stock. Low-cost, diversified, and highly liquid.

EXPENSE RATIO / TER

Annual fund fee as a percentage. VWRA = 0.22%. Active unit trusts = 1.5–2.5%. Always prefer the lowest fee available.

INDEX FUND

A fund that passively tracks a market index (S&P 500, STI). No active stock-picking. Lower cost, historically outperforms most active managers.

INFLATION

The rising cost of goods and services. Singapore averages ~2–3% p.a. Investing is how you protect your purchasing power.

LIQUIDITY

How quickly an investment converts to cash. Stocks = seconds. Property = months. SSBs = redeemable monthly with no penalty.

PORTFOLIO

Your total collection of investments across all accounts. A healthy portfolio is diversified across asset classes and geographies.

REBALANCING

Adjusting your portfolio back to target allocation after market movements. Enforces "buy low, sell high" systematically.

REIT (REAL ESTATE INVESTMENT TRUST)

A company owning income-generating property listed on a stock exchange. Must distribute 90%+ income as dividends. No ABSD required.

SSB (SINGAPORE SAVINGS BOND)

Government-backed bond with stepped interest rates (~3–4%). Capital-guaranteed, redeemable monthly with no penalty. Min $500.

SRS (SUPPLEMENTARY RETIREMENT SCHEME)

Voluntary retirement scheme with dollar-for-dollar income tax relief (up to $15,300/yr for citizens/PRs).

VOLATILITY

How much an investment's price fluctuates. High = crypto. Low = SSBs/CPF. Equities sit in between with a long-term upward trend.

WITHHOLDING TAX (WHT)

Tax deducted before dividends reach you. US charges 30% WHT to Singapore investors. Ireland-domiciled ETFs reduce this to 15%.
